STRATEGY_NAME: DivergenceVolatilityBreakout

STRATEGY_DETAILS:

**Core Concept:** This is a multi-confirmation trend-following strategy designed to capture the initial thrust of a new bullish trend. It waits for a hidden bullish divergence signal (suggesting underlying strength during a pullback) and then requires a decisive breakout above a volatility-based resistance level (Keltner Channel), all occurring within a context of expanding market volatility (Bollinger Bands). This layered approach aims to filter out false breakouts and identify high-probability, momentum-driven entries.

**Required Indicators & Setup:**
1.  **Price:** Candlestick or line chart.
2.  **RSI (Relative Strength Index):** Period 14, applied to close. Used to identify bullish divergence.
3.  **Keltner Channel:** A volatility-based envelope. Typical settings: 20-period EMA (centerline), 1.5 ATR (Average True Range) for the bands.
4.  **Bollinger Bands:** A volatility indicator. Typical settings: 20-period SMA (centerline), 2 standard deviations.
5.  **Ensure all indicators are plotted on the same chart.**

**Key Strategy Components:**
*   **Bullish Divergence (Momentum Precursor):** The RSI makes a higher low while the price makes a lower low or a equal low. This signals weakening selling pressure and is the first condition.
*   **Volatility Expansion (Market Context):** The width between the upper and lower Bollinger Bands must be expanding or be wider than its recent average, indicating increasing market activity and potential for a strong move.
*   **Keltner Channel Breakout (Price Trigger):** The price must close *above* the Upper Keltner Channel band. This is the decisive entry signal, confirming the breakout from a volatility-based dynamic resistance.

**Entry Rules (LONG):**
All of the following conditions must be met on the **same closing candle**:
1.  **Divergence Confirmed:** A valid RSI Bullish Divergence pattern is present (RSI higher low vs. price lower/equal low).
2.  **Volatility Context:** The Bollinger Bands are visibly expanding or are wider than they were 5-10 bars ago.
3.  **Breakout Trigger:** The candle **closes** definitively above the Upper Keltner Channel line.
4.  **Entry Action:** Enter a LONG position at the market price on the close of this confirming candle.

**Exit Rules:**
*   **Profit Target:** The strategy does not specify a fixed take-profit. The trend is intended to be followed until the exit signal occurs.
*   **Stop Loss:** A protective stop-loss should be placed below a recent significant swing low or below the Lower Keltner Channel band. A common method is to use a multiple of the ATR (e.g., 1.5x ATR below entry).
*   **Strategy Exit Signal:** Exit the LONG position when the price **closes below the Upper Keltner Channel** after the initial entry. This signals the immediate bullish momentum has paused or reversed. For a more aggressive trend-follow, you could use a close below the *Middle* Keltner Channel (the 20-period EMA) as the exit.

**Risk Management:**
1.  **Position Sizing:** Risk only a small, fixed percentage of your trading capital per trade (e.g., 1-2%).
2.  **Stop-Loss Discipline:** The stop-loss is mandatory. Calculate your position size based on the distance between your entry price and your stop-loss price.
3.  **Signal Quality:** Avoid entering if the breakout candle is a "wicky" doji or has a very small body; prefer candles with strong bullish bodies for confirmation.
4.  **Market Context:** This strategy performs best in markets transitioning from consolidation/ranges into trending phases. It may generate whipsaws during low-volatility, sideways markets.

**Summary Workflow:**
1.  Identify a potential RSI Bullish Divergence forming during a price dip/pullback.
2.  Monitor the Bollinger Bands for signs of expansion (squeeze ending).
3.  Wait for a candle to close powerfully above the Upper Keltner Channel.
4.  If all three conditions align on the close, ENTER LONG.
5.  Place a stop-loss below support.
6.  Manage the trade: EXIT on a close back below the Upper Keltner Channel.
